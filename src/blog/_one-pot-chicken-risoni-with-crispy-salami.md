  - ¾ tsp paprika
  - ¼ tsp sage powder
  - 2 garlic cloves, minced
  - ½ onion, chopped
  - ¼ cup dry white wine
  - ¼ cup tomato paste
  - 1¼ cups risoni, uncooked
  - 400g canned chickpeas, drained
  - 3 cups chicken stock
  - ¾ cup heavy cream
  - ⅓ cup parmesan, grated, plus extra to serve
  - 150g baby spinach

  - 2 tbsp basil, chopped

## Method

1. Combine salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika, and sage. Coat both sides of the chicken breasts with the spice mix.

2. Heat oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Cook salami batons for 2 minutes, stirring until golden and crispy. Remove to a paper towel-lined bowl, leaving the rendered fat in the pan.

3. In the same pan, cook the spiced chicken for 3 minutes per side until golden and cooked through. Remove to a plate.

4. Cook garlic and onion in the pan for 1½ minutes until translucent. Add wine and simmer, scraping up any fond, until mostly evaporated.

5. Stir in tomato paste for 1 minute. Add risoni, chickpeas, stock, salt, and pepper.

6. Bring to a simmer and cook for 8 minutes, stirring frequently. Lower heat as the mixture thickens.

7. Stir in cream, parmesan, spinach, and sun-dried tomatoes until the spinach wilts. Remove from heat.

8. Slice the rested chicken. Divide risoni into bowls, top with sliced chicken, crispy salami, extra parmesan, and chopped basil.
