## Method

1. Mix soy sauce, Chinese cooking wine, sugar, and chilli garlic sauce together in a small bowl to make the sauce.

2. Trim green beans and chop into 2–2.5cm pieces.

3. Heat peanut oil in a wok or large skillet over very high heat. Add beans and char for 2½ minutes total, stirring periodically, until blistered but still tender-crisp. Remove and set aside.

4. In the same pan, add onion, garlic, and ginger; sauté for 1 minute until edges are golden.

5. Add pork mince and cook on high heat, breaking it up, for about 2 minutes until cooked through.

6. Pour in sauce and stir for 30 seconds. Return beans to the pan and stir for another 30 seconds.

7. Serve immediately over rice, mixing everything together and eating with a spoon.
